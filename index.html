Research Projects

Advanced Alloy Design: Defect Engineering in Complex Systems (2023 IRG2)

Illinois Materials Research Science and Engineering Center (I-MRSEC) | Jun. 2023 - Present

Led computational alloy design research focusing on energy prediction and defect thermodynamics in multi-component systems including high entropy alloys and disordered oxides. Developed first-principles frameworks to predict formation energies and defect equilibria in complex alloy systems, enabling rational design of materials with tailored transport and mechanical properties. Established quantitative structure-property relationships linking alloy composition, defect concentration, and macroscopic properties through integrated computational approaches.

Computational Modeling of Defect Equilibria in Œ≤-Ga2O3

The Air Force Office of Scientific Research | Jun. 2021 - Dec. 2024

Created breakthrough approaches for commercialization through advanced defect engineering, providing unprecedented control over electronic properties in power devices. Developed comprehensive computational frameworks and MATLAB tools (KROGER) for quantitative modeling of defect concentrations in wide bandgap semiconductors, enabling precise prediction of electronic properties under various growth conditions.

MURI: Œ≤-Ga2O3 as a High-Critical Field Strength Material for Power Systems

The Air Force Office of Scientific Research | Jun. 2021 - Dec. 2024

Investigated defect diffusion mechanisms in Œ≤-Ga2O3 using first-principles calculations and master diffusion equations. Established optimal doping strategies by determining formation energies and diffusion characteristics of acceptors and donors using first-principles calculations.
